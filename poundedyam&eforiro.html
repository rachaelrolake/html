Pounded Yam & Eforiiro

Pounded Yam is a staple in many west African homes, but not usually eaten bt itself, it is often paired with many delicious African soups and stew.

It is a classy nigerian dish, is a smooth and stretchy dough made from boiled yams. It is mostly eaten by the yoruba people,often served during special occassions.

Efo riro is a vegetable soup and native soup of the yoruba people of western nigeria usually eaten with swallow. In this case we would be making pounded yam and efo riro

what you need for pounded yam

Ingredients for efo riro

  • 2 bunches of Ugwu leaves
  • 3 medium size bell peppers
  • 2 scotch bonnet
  • Assorted meat
  • Dried stock fish
  • 1 tablespoon locust beans
  • Seasoning cubes
  • 3 tablespoons ground crayfish
  • Salt to taste

Steps to prepare ponded yam

  1. Peel the yam
  2. Cut into small pieces
  3. Wash the yam with clean water
  4. Put inside a pot add water and cook foe 20-25 minutes
  5. Use motar and pestle to pound the yaminto a fine paste
  6. When done, package into a fine paste with white serving leather
  7. Store in cooler to kep it hot
