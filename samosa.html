Samosa

Samosa is a South Asian pastry with savoury filling including ingredients such as spiced potatoes, onions, peas, meat or fish. it is a very popular snack in Nepalese cusine.

It may take different forms, including triangular, cone, or half-moon shapes, depending on the region. It cann be eaten as side dish, and also served in various ocassion. Sother names for samosa are: Sambusa, Samusa, Singara/sinara

ingredients for Samosa dough

  • 120gr all purpose flour (Plain white flour)
  • 3 tablespoonof vegetable oil
  • Half teaspoon dry parsley, thyme
  • Half levelled teaspon of salt
  • Warm water

Ingredients for the filling:

  • 200gr minced chicken
  • 2 medium Irish potatoes
  • 2 handfuls of green peas
  • 1 long stalk of spring onion or minced onion
  • 1 tiny garlic/a pinch of garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoonof curry powder
  • A pinch of black pepper(optional)
  • 1 stock cube
  • 2 cooking spoonfuls of vegetable oil
  • Chili pepper
  • Salt
  • vegetable oil for deep-frying the Samosas

steps to make the filling:

  1. Cut the spring onions into thin circles and set aside
  2. Wash and peel the Irish potatoes and carrots. cut into very tiny bits and cook in salted water for 5 minutes. If using fresh peas, add it to the carrots and potatoes and cook for 5 more minutes until all the vegetables are soft. Strain and set aside
  3. Heat up the oil in a pot, add the spring onions, garlic, curry, black pepper and stir-fry for few seconds
  4. Add the minced chicken. stir-fry until when done
  5. Add the stock cubes, chili pepper, salt to taste. Then add the cooked vegetables. Mix thorougly, Set aside and leave to cool.

steps to make the dough:

  1. Pour the flour into a bowl, Add salt and add the 3 tablespoonful of vegetable oil ans warm water. Mix` well until you get a stretchy dough.
  2. Then cover with an airtihgt plastic wrap or aluminium foil and leave to rest for about 15 minutes.
  3. After 15 minutes, knead the dough to make it more streachy and elastic.
  4. Cut out a bit, roll out with tolling pin. Make sure its not so thin.
  5. Using a circular cutter, cut the rolled dough, and remove the excess dough at the corners, and then cut the circular shape into two diameters
  6. Rub water on the straight edges of one of the dough and shape it into a cone; take a spoonful of samosa filling and scoop it into the cone shaped dough.
  7. Dont over fill it. Rub some water at theedges and seal tightly.
  8. Repeat the process for the remaining dough
